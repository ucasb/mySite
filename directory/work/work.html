3DEO, Inc. - Prototype Lab Manager

Optical Goniometer / Contact Angle Analyzer

3DEO, Inc. - Torrance, CA

Fixture for using a camera phone to measure the contact angle of 3DEO's prioritary binder solution, used to keep metal powder together during the 3D printing process. BitSpace Chicago - Product Engineer & Makerspace Guide

Chicago, IL / March 2020 - August 2021

Bitspace is a youth-focused makerspace that gives students access to professional manufacturing software and tools via weekly programming that turned completely virtual in 2020. I personally taught CAD, electronics, robotics, game design, programming, you name it.

BitSpace - Chicago, IL

Self-contained kit for kids ages 10+, designed during first COVID lockdowns as an alternative to in-person instruction. Tested during virtual BitSpace summer camps, and later distributed to Chicago Public Schools. The challenge was designing a kid-proof electronics kit for a wide age range, without requiring any soldering or fine motor skills.See more ->

Laser Cut Pinball

BitSpace - Chicago, IL

Another kit designed for virtual summer courses, for younger learners. Made of laser cut parts, dowels, rubber bands and corner brackets. Kids had the option of customing their game with their own laser cut obstacles and paddles using InkScape. Their vector art would be cleaned up by a guide (me), laser cut in-house, and mailed out at the end of the session.

Circuit-Bent Bible

Commisioned by a friend in Chicago, IL

Commisioned circuit bend, schematic taken from Look Mum No Computer. 8 bi-directional switches activate a 16-sample bank, which can be configured to be latched or momentary. Samples are stored in a microSD card, loaded with the entirety of the Bible by default, but able to be loaded with any .wav files. Controls include sample selection, pitch modification, and a glitch/stutter effect. Wish I could've kept this one.

Salmon - Apology2 Music Video

Made for/with singer-songwriter Salmon in Oberlin, OH

My first, only, and most likely last music video, made for the fun of it at age 19. Filmed over a single day in North-East Ohio day using equipment sourced from friends. Captured on a Sony Hi8 camcorder, processed with a 1991 Panasonic WJ-MX50 video mixer, and imported with a cheap-o Elgato RCA capture card, which should explain why the quality tops out at 480p. The dolly shot was accomplished by lying prone on the roof of the borrowed car we used to find different filming locations, which were just nearby abandoned farms and factories. Super fun to make, and I'm happy with how it turned out considering a budget and experience level of absolute zero.
